Customer Intelligence

The three themes most consistently surfaced across reviews (Yelp, 4.5★/62):

Top praise theme 1 — Fast response time

Reviewers repeatedly cite rapid arrival, with on-site response noted within roughly 10 minutes. This speed is the brand's signature operational strength.

Top praise theme 2 — Reliable long-term "go-to" locksmith

Customers describe RSM Lock & Safe as their reliable, repeat "go-to" locksmith — a relationship business built on returning customers, not one-off calls.

Top praise theme 3 — Owner Bob Slama personally provides service

Reviews emphasize that owner Bob Slama personally does the work (sometimes alongside his son), and read him as professional, knowledgeable, and trustworthy — a named-local-owner advantage over call-center dispatch competitors.

Recurring friction point

The one recurring concern noted across reviews is variable trip / service-call fees — occasional surprise at the cost of the call-out. Transparent, upfront pricing on the website would defuse this directly.

Where RSM leads vs. lags. RSM Lock & Safe leads on the things that are hardest to fake: a genuinely 38-year local track record, a named owner-operator (Bob Slama) who personally does the work, a strong 4.5★ Yelp base across 62 reviews, and a fast-response reputation — credibility a newer competitor cannot buy. Where it lags is entirely fixable presentation and coverage: it trails Ben'z badly on review volume (62 vs. 346) and Google visibility (0 vs. an active profile), and it is structurally narrower — weekday-only hours, no 24/7 or after-hours coverage, and no automotive/car-key service, all of which Ben'z, Leonard, and RSM Lake advertise. It also displays no license/"Licensed & Bonded" badge, while Leonard makes its license a headline. The strategic read: RSM is the trusted incumbent that is simply under-marketed — competitors are winning on availability claims and discoverability, not on reputation.
