Business Overview

Electric Medics, owned by Mike Parks, is a residential electrical contractor based at 24000 Alicia Parkway, Ste 17-241, Mission Viejo, CA 92691, reachable at (949) 462-9200. The company has served South Orange County since 1995 — roughly 31 years, though the BBB profile lists a business-start date of 10/25/1996 — and operates as a residential-only specialist across a broad service area that includes Lake Forest, Rancho Santa Margarita, San Juan Capistrano, the Laguna communities, Coto de Caza, and Dana Point. Its catalog spans residential electrical repairs, electrical panel replacement and main/sub-panel upgrades, EV home-charger and 220V installations, remodel and dedicated-circuit wiring, recessed and security lighting, ceiling-fan and chandelier installation, bathroom exhaust fans, circuit-breaker replacement, GFI installation, smart-home technology implementation, and 24/7 emergency electrical service.

Digital Presence Audit

Website — electricmedics.com  ·  Quality 3 / 5  ·  Wix (mobile-responsive)

The site is live, mobile-responsive, and scores 3/5 on build quality. It leads with the headline “Your 5-Star Electrician in Mission Viejo, CA” and lists a deep, well-organized 17-item service menu.

What works

  • Strong trust stack: CSLB License C-10 729017; licensed, bonded & insured
  • “Since 1995” longevity and a perfect 5-star claim across Google & Yelp
  • HomeAdvisor screened-and-approved badge; background-checked techs
  • Satisfaction guarantee — “We will not leave the job until you’re satisfied”
  • Comprehensive, scannable service list (EV charging, panels, smart home)

What does not

  • Single weak CTA (“Book Now”) with a soft above-the-fold conversion path
  • No visible email-capture or lead-magnet — phone or booking form only
  • No named manufacturer certifications (e.g., Tesla, Lutron) despite EV focus
  • No published response-time or free-estimate promise to anchor urgency
  • Wix template look trails the more modern competitor sites in the market

Where Electric Medics leads — and where it lags

Leads: Electric Medics leads on rating quality and tenure: a flawless 5.0 sustained over ~30 years, with owner Mike and his crew named so often that trust is effectively pre-sold — an asset none of the larger competitors can manufacture quickly.

Lags: Where it lags is reach and conversion mechanics: its 117 Yelp reviews are dwarfed by ASL (516) and JPS (402); it advertises no named manufacturer certifications or response-time guarantee that JPS uses to win EV and high-end jobs; and its Wix site offers a single soft “Book Now” CTA against the multi-form, badge-forward pages of JPS and OC Certified Electric. The reputation is best-in-class — the storefront and review volume have not caught up to it.

Branch & Root Recommendation

Recommended Service Tier
Customer Reactivation Engine ⚡

Thirty-plus years of residential customers and 117+ five-star Yelp reviews signal a large, loyal past-customer base — but there is no visible email capture, CRM, or automated follow-up to mobilize it. A reactivation engine converts that decade-deep goodwill into repeat panel, EV-charger, and remodel jobs: capture the existing list, segment by service history, and run automated follow-up and seasonal-maintenance touches that turn one-time customers into a recurring revenue stream.

Estimated revenue opportunity

Reactivating even a small slice of a 30-year, 100%-recommend customer base into recurring panel/EV/remodel work — jobs that routinely run into the thousands of dollars each — represents a high-margin, five-figure-plus annual upside with near-zero new-customer-acquisition cost.
