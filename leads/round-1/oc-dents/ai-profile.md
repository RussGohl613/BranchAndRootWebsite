## 4. Before / After Recipe

The before/after is the brand's highest-performing format. A convincing pair sells the paintless promise: *same panel, same paint, dent simply gone.*

**Hard rules for a credible pair**
1. **Identical camera angle, framing, and distance** in both frames. Lock it.
2. **Identical lighting and time of day** — same sun position, same shadows, same reflections in the surrounding glass.
3. **Same panel, same car, same color** — only the dent changes. No new scratches, no relocated trim, no different wheels.
4. **Reflection lines tell the story:**
   - *Before* — the reflected line (sky edge, garage door, reflection-board stripe) **bends, breaks, or warps** over the dent.
   - *After* — that same reflected line runs **perfectly straight and continuous.** This is the proof.
5. **Factory paint stays intact in both** — gloss, orange-peel texture, and color identical. Never show primer, filler, sanding, or fresh paint.
6. Optional: include the **PDR reflection board** in the "before" to make the dent obvious, then a clean glossy panel in the "after."

**Layout:** vertical split (left/right) or stacked (top/bottom) with small orange "BEFORE" / "AFTER" labels. Keep the dividing line clean. Add a one-line caption: vehicle + city + time ("Door ding · Irvine · 35 min").

**Generation note:** when using AI image-to-image, generate the *after* by repairing the *same* source frame (or by prompting "identical shot, dent removed, reflection lines now straight, factory paint untouched") so the pair stays plausibly real.
