Privacy Policy

— Last updated

May 2026

Branch and Root Consulting (“Branch & Root,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) operates the website at branchandrootconsulting.com (the “Site”). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect information when you visit our Site or contact us through it.

We take your privacy seriously and are committed to handling your information responsibly. This policy applies to all visitors and prospective clients who interact with us through this website.

1. Information We Collect

Information you provide directly

When you submit a consultation request or book an appointment through our Site, we collect:

  • Full name
  • Business name
  • Phone number
  • Email address
  • City and service area
  • Industry or business type
  • Optional: how you heard about us, and a description of your current business challenge

You are not required to provide any information to browse our Site. The above information is only collected when you voluntarily submit it through our consultation request form or booking calendar.

Information collected automatically

Like most websites, our Site may collect certain technical information automatically when you visit, including:

  • IP address (anonymized)
  • Browser type and version
  • Device type
  • Pages visited and time spent on pages
  • Referring URL (the page that linked you to our Site)

This information is collected through analytics tools (described below) and is used only in aggregate to understand how visitors use our Site. We do not use it to identify individual visitors.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information you provide solely to:

  • Respond to your consultation request or confirm your booking
  • Schedule and conduct your free consultation call
  • Follow up with you about our services if you have expressed interest
  • Maintain our internal client relationship records (CRM) if you become a client

We do not use your information to send unsolicited marketing emails or add you to automated marketing sequences without your consent. If you submit a consultation request, you may receive one follow-up to confirm or schedule your call — nothing more unless you engage further.

3. Analytics and Tracking Technologies

We use or plan to use the following third-party analytics and advertising tools on this Site. Where a tool is listed but not yet active, it is noted below.

Google Analytics 4

We use Google Analytics 4 (“GA4”) to understand how visitors navigate our Site in aggregate. GA4 collects anonymized data about page views, session duration, and general traffic sources. It does not collect personally identifiable information unless you submit a form. You can opt out of GA4 tracking by using the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

Meta Pixel (Facebook/Instagram)

We use or plan to use the Meta Pixel to measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns run on Facebook and Instagram. The Meta Pixel records certain page actions (such as viewing a page or submitting a form) and may set cookies on your browser. You can manage your ad preferences through Facebook’s Ad Preferences or opt out of interest-based advertising at aboutads.info.

Google Ads

We may use Google Ads conversion tracking to measure whether a consultation booking results from a Google Ads campaign. This involves a tag that fires on our booking confirmation page and reports an anonymized conversion event to Google. No personally identifiable information is shared with Google through this tag.

Cookies

Our Site may use cookies placed by the third-party tools described above (Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, Google Ads). These are standard analytics and advertising cookies. You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies will not prevent you from using any functionality on our Site.

4. How We Store and Protect Your Information

Information submitted through our consultation form or booking calendar is stored within our Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system, which is hosted by GoHighLevel. GoHighLevel maintains industry-standard security practices including data encryption at rest and in transit.

We do not store credit card numbers or payment information on our systems.

We retain consultation inquiry data for as long as necessary to respond to your request and, if applicable, to maintain our client relationship. You may request deletion of your data at any time (see Section 6).

5. Information Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties.

We may share your information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers: We use GoHighLevel as our CRM and scheduling platform. Your submitted information is stored and processed within their platform. GoHighLevel operates under its own privacy policy and does not sell data to advertisers.
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose information if required to do so by law or in response to a valid legal request.
  • Business transfer: In the unlikely event that Branch and Root Consulting is acquired or merged with another entity, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We would notify you before your information is transferred and becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

6. Your Rights Under California Law (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) gives you specific rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right to Know: You have the right to request that we disclose what personal information we have collected about you, the categories of sources from which it was collected, the business purpose for collecting it, and any third parties with whom it has been shared.
  • Right to Delete: You have the right to request that we delete any personal information we have collected about you, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA rights. We will not deny you services, charge you different prices, or provide a different level of service because you exercised your privacy rights.
  • Right to Opt Out of Sale: We do not sell your personal information. There is nothing to opt out of.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at the email address listed below. We will respond to verifiable consumer requests within 45 days.

7. Children’s Privacy

Our Site is directed to business owners and is not intended for children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such information, please contact us immediately and we will delete it.

8. Links to Third-Party Sites

Our Site may contain links to third-party websites (such as our email provider or social media profiles). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites and encourage you to review their privacy policies independently.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. When we make material changes, we will update the “Last updated” date at the top of this page. Continued use of our Site after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

10. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, want to exercise your data rights, or have a privacy-related concern, please contact us:
